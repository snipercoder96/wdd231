<h2>Basic Movements</h2>
            <article>
                <h3>Pawns</h3>
                <p>Pawns move forward one square, with the option to move two squares on their first move. They capture
                    diagonally.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Rooks</h3>
                <p>Rooks move horizontally or vertically across any number of squares.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Knights</h3>
                <p>Knights move in an L-shape: two squares in one direction, then one square perpendicular. They can jump over
                    pieces.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Bishops</h3>
                <p>Bishops move diagonally across any number of squares.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Queens</h3>
                <p>The queen combines the power of the rook and bishop, moving horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Kings</h3>
                <p>The king moves one square in any direction. Protecting the king is the ultimate goal of the game.</p>
            </article>


<h2>Special Rules</h2>
            <article>
                <h3>Castling</h3>
                <p>A move involving the king and rook for defense and development.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>En Passant</h3>
                <p>A special pawn capture that can occur immediately after an opponent moves a pawn two squares forward.</p>
            </article>
            <article>
                <h3>Promotion</h3>
                <p>When a pawn reaches the opposite side of the board, it can be promoted to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
                </p>
            </article>
