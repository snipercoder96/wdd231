PawnSchool

The name PawnSchool represents the journey of a pawn in chess: starting small, learning step by step, and eventually “graduating” into a stronger piece either a knight, bishop, queen or rook. It reflects the teaching philosophy of the site.

Site Purpose

PawnSchool provides an interactive hub for chess learners. The site offers teaching tools, GIF previews of moves, embedded studies, and resources to help players understand openings, tactics, and endgames. It blends visual storytelling with structured lessons to make chess learning engaging.

Scenarios

  • “How do I learn the Queen’s Gambit step by step?”
  • “Where can I watch interactive lessons on chess openings?”
  • “How can i learn how to play chess”

Color Scheme

  • chess background image
  • --chess-dark-gray (#2c2c2c) – modal background
  • --chess-white (#ffffff) – links, text, headings
  • --chess-gold (#d4af37) – accents, hover effects for buttons/nav
  • --chess-green (#228B22) – gradient backgrounds
  • --chess-light-gray (#f5f5f5) – section backgrounds, secondary text

Typography

  • Merriweather (serif) – used for headings to give a scholarly, classic feel.
  • Roboto (sans-serif) – used for body text for clarity and modern readability.
